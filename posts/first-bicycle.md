## Rethinking Locomotion

When I decided to shift my lifestyle from the fast lane of corporate strategy to something more grounded, the first thing I audited was my commute. Cars represent independence in the modern world, but they also represent a tether—to gas prices, to mechanics, to external grids. 

I wanted to find a way to move that relied entirely on my own energy. Enter: The Touring Bicycle.

### The Unboxing

The box arrived looking like it had already survived a tour. Inside was the frame that will hopefully become my primary mode of transport.

*   **Steel Frame:** Chosen for durability and repairability. If it cracks in a remote village, anyone with a welding torch can fix it. You can't say the same for carbon fiber.
*   **Mechanical Disc Brakes:** Again, favoring systems I can maintain myself.
*   **Dynamo Hub:** To generate electricity while I ride, powering lights and eventually charging battery banks for my devices.

Building the bike from the frame up was an exercise in understanding exactly how the machine works. It felt like debugging code, but with grease instead of `console.log`.
